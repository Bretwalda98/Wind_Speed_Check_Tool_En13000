Method (EN 13000) — concise

Formula. \( V_{calc} = V_{max,TAB} \cdot \sqrt{\dfrac{1.2\; m_{hL}}{A_w}} \), with \( A_w = A_p\, c_w \).

  • 1.2 m²/Te is the EN 13000 constant; do not alter.
  • Aw includes shape effects via cw.
  • Vperm = min(Vcalc, Vmax,TAB).

Typical cw values. Plate/cube 1.1–2.0, cylinder 0.6–1.0, ball 0.3–0.4, hemisphere (front) 0.2–0.3, hemisphere (rear) 0.8–1.2, wind rotor ≈1.6. Use manufacturer data when available.

This tool is a simplification specific to lifting per EN 13000. It is not a substitute for full wind-action assessment per EN 1991-1-4 (Eurocode) for permanent structures.

WindCheck – Help & How-to

What this tool does

Calculates the permissible wind speed for a lifting operation per EN 13000, considering the lifted mass, the effective wind area of the load, and the crane’s duty-chart limit.

When to use it

  • During lift planning and pre-lift checks.
  • To assess changes in load shape/area, rigging, or mass.
  • To compare forecast/site wind to the governing permissible wind.

Inputs required

  • Vmax,TAB (m/s): From the crane manufacturer’s duty chart for the current configuration.
  • mhL (Te): Total lifted mass (hook + rigging + load).
  • Ap (m²): Projected area of the lifted body normal to the wind.
  • cw (–): Resistance coefficient for the shape (manufacturer value preferred).
  • Operational wind (optional, m/s): Measured or forecast site wind for a Go/No-go indication.

Choosing cw (quick guide)

Hemisphere (rear)0.8 – 1.2 (≈1.0)

If a manufacturer provides a specific value, use that instead of the presets.

Step-by-step

  1. Enter Vmax,TAB from the duty chart.
  2. Enter mhL, Ap, and choose or type cw.
    Effective area is computed as Aw = Ap × cw.
  3. The app calculates Vcalc = Vmax,TAB × √((1.2 × mhL) / Aw).
  4. The governing permissible wind is Vperm = min(Vcalc, Vmax,TAB).
  5. (Optional) Enter site wind to see the Go/No-go pill.

Worked examples

Example 1 — mhL=65 Te, Ap=200 m², cw=1.4 → Aw=280 m², Vmax,TAB=11.1 m/s.
Result: Vcalc=5.86 m/s → Vperm=5.86 m/s (limited by load/area).

Example 2 — mhL=85 Te, Ap=50 m², cw=1.2 → Aw=60 m², Vmax,TAB=9.0 m/s.
Result: Vcalc=11.73 m/s → Vperm=9.0 m/s (limited by duty chart).

Interpreting results

  • Aw: Effective area exposed to wind.
  • Vcalc: Permissible wind from EN 13000 formula for your load.
  • Vperm: The number that governs (never exceed this). Shown also in mph & knots.
  • Go/No-go: Quick check comparing your input wind to Vperm.
